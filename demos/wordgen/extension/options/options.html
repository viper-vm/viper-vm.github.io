WordGen settings

Provider

WordGen works out of the box with the free local engine. Bring your own API key to unlock every mode — the key stays in your browser and is only ever sent to the provider you pick.

Runs entirely in your browser using a built-in lexicon and word embeddings. Great for synonyms, grammar touch-ups and quick rewrites; the AI-heavy modes (prompts, emails, expand) need a provider above.

Any OpenAI-compatible chat-completions endpoint works: Groq, Ollama (http://localhost:11434/v1/chat/completions), Gemini's compatibility endpoint, and more. Leave the endpoint blank to use OpenAI itself.

WordGen POSTs JSON like {"action":"transform","mode":"paraphrase","text":"…","opts":{}} and expects {"options":[{"text":"…","note":"…"}]} back (or {"candidates":[…]} for synonyms). Saving asks Chrome for permission to reach this host.

When on, the key is stored in your browser profile and synced via your Google account, so it follows you between machines. Untick to keep it on this device only.

Appearance

The bubble is a small round “W” that appears near selected text — click it to open the note prefilled, or to jump straight to synonyms for a single word.

History

0 saved transforms on this device (the newest 100 are kept). History never leaves your browser.

Privacy: WordGen has no server. Your text is sent only to the provider you configure (or nowhere at all with the local engine), and your key is stored only in Chrome's extension storage.
